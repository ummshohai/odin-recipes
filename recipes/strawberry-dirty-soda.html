Strawberry Dirty Soda

A Fresh Way to Start the Summer

A simple DIY drink made from strawberries that'll sweeten up everyone's summer. Gathering a classic with a whipped cream spin to sip one of the bubbliest and creamiest drinks you'll ever have.

Ingredients

Strawberry Syrup
  • 1 pound fresh strawberries (trimmed)
  • 1/2 cups sugar, divided
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon butter extract (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • pinch salt
For each dirty soda
  • 2 ounces cream soda
  • 6 ounces strawberry sparkling water
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons Strawberry Shortcake Syrup
  • 1 tablespoon half and half
  • 1 heaping tablespoon whipped cream
  • 1 teaspoon chopped dehydrated strawberries

Directions

Step 1

Combine strawberries and 1 cup sugar in a bowl and mix with a spoon. Cover and let sit on the counter for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.

Step 2

Add 1 cup of water to the macerated strawberries and stir everything together. Transfer into a saucepan and add the rest of the sugar. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally. Once simmering, lower heat to medium-low. Simmer until strawberries are very soft, stirring occasionally, 20 to 25 minutes. After 10 minutes you can smash the strawberries with a potato masher which will produce a slightly thicker, “fruitier” syrup.

Step 3

Remove from heat and allow the pan to cool for 15 minutes before straining the strawberry mixture through a fine mesh strainer.

2 Ways for Straining

Method 1: place a strainer over a tall pot and let all the syrup drain through the strawberries, stirring occasionally, which will produce a clearer and thinner syrup.

Method 2: Use the back of a spoon or ladle to squeeze the syrup through the strainer into a bowl, which will result in some of the strawberry being pushed through, creating a thicker syrup. Stir in vanilla extract, butter extract, almond extract, and a very small pinch of salt. Transfer into a smaller container, and store in the fridge until needed.

Step 4

To make each dirty soda, fill a glass with ice, and pour in 2 ounces of cream soda. Fill the glass the rest of the way with strawberry sparkling water, or strawberry soda if you want something sweeter, leaving a half inch unfilled at the top. Pour in 3 to 4 tablespoons strawberry syrup, and stir everything with a spoon for a few seconds. Top of with a splash of half and half. Garnish with a dollop of whipped cream and sprinkle with chopped dehydrated strawberries or chopped fresh strawberries.

For Butter Extract

Combine 1/4 cup unsalted butter and 1/4 cup 80 proof vodka in a small saucepan over low hea. Stir continuously until butter is completely melted into the vodka. Do not let the mixture boil. Leave over lowest heat for 5 minutes, stirring continuously. Remove from heat, cover with a lid, and let it sit at room temperature for 8 hours. Transfer the mixture to an airtight container and freeze overnight. The fat will freeze while the infused vodka remains liquid beneath. Remove it from the freezer the next day and strain through a cheesecloth lined fine-mesh sieve. Squeeze the cheesecloth to extract all the liquid. You will get about 5 tablespoons butter extract. Store butter extract in an airtight glass bottle in the refrigerator.
